Experiences

  • Research Project | Laboratoire Informatique Avignon (LIA) | Avignon, France | 2nd Year Undergraduate Intern | 2 mo. 2021

    I worked under the supervision of Associate Prof Titouan Parcollet (LIA and Adjunct Researcher at Cambridge University).

    I have investigated different ways of making LiGRU a mandatory alternative to LSTM/GRU for speech processing. There is theoretical and empirical evidence that LiGRU simply is better than LSTM and GRU. Unfortunately, LiGRU suffers from poor GPU implementation and instability in the recurrent connection.

    I reduced by 4x the computation time of the LiGRU by implementing from scratch the Backpropagation Through Time equations in PyTorch with torch.autograd.Function and by combining torch.jit.script and Cuda custom kernels with CuPy.

    The work has led to a pull request (currently being reviewed) into SpeechBrain which is an open-source and all-in-one conversational AI toolkit with more than 4k stars on Github.

    Stack used : Python, C++ (Cuda) - PyTorch, CuPy, Numba, Triton (openAI), OVH, Docker.

  • Research Project | √âcole Polytechnique F√©d√©rale de Lausanne (EPFL) | (Remote) Avignon, France | Ongoing Project

    This project is a collaboration between Laurent Aeschbach (GoergiaTech) and the EPFL for the sponsoring of Data & GPUs.

    We first study the literature associated with EMG/sEMG linked to the prediction of finger angles.

    The sEMGs are sensitive to the factors of intra-variability and inter-variability which are a major bottleneck in the current research state.   • Research Project | E-Magina | Avignon, France | 1st Year Undergraduate Intern | 1 mo. 2020

    I have been asked to conduct extensive data scrapping, analyses, and modeling of an online french sports club directory. One other aspect of the work was to carefully design and experiment with a System Multi-Agent to automatically classify a club description with the right associated sports.

    The idea behind it was to create the first accessible french directory that could be used to search for a sports club easily contrary to the oldest available website that was not designed for this purpose. Only the name, city, and a short description of the sports club were provided in the database.

    We showed that by using a System-Multi Agent (SMA) in the context of a low-resource dataset that the SMA was by a large margin better than a Recurrent Neural Networks in this specific context.

    Stack used : Python - BeautifulSoup 4, Pandas, Nltk and Spacy.
